The Travel Corporation – Power of One Project

Consolidating 40+ brands into one B2C ecommerce engine

The impact

  • Reduced time-to-market for brand migrations by 60%
  • Improved baseline conversion by 12% across pilot brands
  • Cut design and engineering duplication costs by £2M annually
  • Established the first enterprise-wide design system supporting 2M+ guests per year

My role: Principal Product Designer (Strategic Lead). I defined the UX architecture, led the cross-functional task force, built the design system from scratch, and secured C-suite buy-in for the "clean sheet" approach across 40+ brand stakeholders.

Image: System overview or dashboard

The problem we were solving

The organisation ran 40+ brand websites built for mixed B2B and B2C needs.

This created:

  • Fragmented customer journeys with 300%+ variation in conversion rates
  • Inconsistent quality and duplicated effort across brands
  • 18-month lead times for new features
  • Poor performance, accessibility, and SEO outcomes

PO1 was created to fix this at a system level.

The goal was not to redesign a website. It was to build a repeatable B2C ecommerce capability that could scale.

Programme intent

PO1 set out to do three things:

  1. Move the business to an ecommerce-first model
  2. Create one consolidated platform and design system
  3. Enable faster, data-led iteration across brands

This required changes to technology, design practice, team structure, and governance.

The programme was brand-agnostic by design.

My leadership challenge: The "clean sheet" principle was the hardest sell. It required navigating 10+ years of legacy pride from individual brand stakeholders. I used analytics audits to prove that legacy patterns were actively costing the business revenue, which shifted the conversation from opinion to evidence.

Image: Before/after comparison or analytics

Guiding principles

I established five principles that shaped every decision:

  • Start from a clean sheet
  • Design for B2C only
  • Optimise for performance and conversion first
  • Validate assumptions early with real users
  • Build once, reuse everywhere

This avoided incremental improvement of legacy patterns.

Building the design system

I built the multi-brand design system from scratch.

The challenge: Create one system that could serve 40+ brands without destroying their individual identities.

My approach:

  • Separated structure from style using a token architecture
  • Created a core component library (40 components)
  • Built brand-specific theme layers
  • Established clear governance and contribution models

Result: New brand migrations dropped from 18 months to 6 weeks. Design-to-development handoff time reduced by 70%.

Key outcomes

Business impact

Team impact

  • Established first enterprise-wide design system
  • Created repeatable discovery and validation process
  • Reduced design-to-development handoff time by 70%
  • Enabled one design team to support 40+ brands

Customer impact

  • 28% improvement in findability
  • 15% reduction in bounce rate
  • 18% increase in return visit conversion
  • 12% increase in checkout completion

Why this approach mattered

PO1 was designed to scale beyond a single launch.

The structure allowed:

  • Multiple brands to migrate safely
  • Patterns to be validated once and reused everywhere
  • Teams to move faster without lowering quality

It created the foundation for long-term B2C growth, not a one-off redesign.

My contribution: I defined the UX architecture, built the design system, led the cross-functional task force, and managed stakeholder alignment across 40+ brands. I established the principles, processes, and governance model that allowed this programme to succeed at scale.
